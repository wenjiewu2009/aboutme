Overview

Wenjie Wu is an Associate Professor at the Urban Institute, Heriot-Watt University. He did his PhD from the London School of Economics and Political Science (LSE) and previously worked as a Lecturer in the Urban Studies, University of Glasgow. He remains as a research affiliate at the Spatial Economics Research Centre, LSE, a co-investigator of the ESRC-funded Urban Big Data Center (University of Glasgow), and an affiliated chair professor in Ji Nan University (Guangzhou, China). He is a board member of the review committee of Asia-Pacific Network for Housing Research (2016) and is going to chair the European Chapter of the International Association of China Planning from 2017.Dr Wu's main research areas are socio-spatial implications of local and regional development, urban big data and built environment (mainly from wellbeing, real estate, and infrastructure perspective). Dr Wu has been co-editing two special issues in journals incl. Housing, Theory and Society, and Urban Policy and Research. His work has been widely published or forthcoming in a variety of journals including Annals of the Association of American Geographers, Journal of Regional Science, Papers in Regional Science, Land Use Policy, Urban Studies, Journal of Real Estate Finance and Economics, Journal of Comparative Economics, etc. His work has been funded by a range of international funders incl. ESRC, NSFC, Lincoln Land Institute and etc. Dr Wu has supervised numerous projects for World Bank, China Development Bank and other non-profit and governmental organizations.

Prizes

Dr Wu has received excellent teaching evaluations on a number of occasions and best paper awards from international leading conferences, incl. International Regional Science Association. He has been awarded as the World Social Science Fellow (Big data in urban contexts) by the International Social Science Council in 2015.
